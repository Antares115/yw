About

The story behind the work.

My path into design wasn't a straight line. I studied Psychology at Simon Fraser University, later earned my Associate of Arts at Coquitlam College, and spent my early career working in administration. My role as Office Administrator went far beyond paperwork — I designed posters, refreshed visual materials, and contributed to rebuilding the company website.

Somewhere between aligning a headline and choosing the right photograph, I realized design wasn't just a task on my to-do list; it was the part of the day I looked forward to. That realization led me to BCIT, where I'm completing my Graphic Design Certificate. My focus is poster and promotional design, visual communication, and image post-production — making everyday materials look sharp, clear, and worth a second look.

I believe good design should feel honest before it feels clever. I'm bilingual in English and Mandarin, I bring a psychology background that quietly shapes how I think about audiences, and — outside of design — I'm a proud cat owner with a soft spot for animal welfare. Small reminders that the best work usually comes from caring about something.
