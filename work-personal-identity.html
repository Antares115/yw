Case Study · Personal Branding

Personal Brand Identity

"Design with heart, built with clarity."

A complete personal brand built to introduce me before I ever say a word — defining who I am as a designer, then translating that into a calm, cohesive visual system.

Role

Brand strategy, logo, visual system & collateral — solo project

Tools

Illustrator, Photoshop, Canva

Year

2025 · BCIT Graphic Design

Cover / brand appliedimages/identity-hero.png
01

Overview

As a designer stepping into the industry, I needed a personal brand that could speak for me — on a portfolio, a business card, a LinkedIn page. The goal wasn't a trendy logo, but a coherent identity rooted in who I am and how I work.

The objective: define a clear brand message and tone of voice, then translate it into a flexible visual system — logo, color, and typography — and apply it consistently across print and digital touchpoints.

02

Discovery

I started by exploring who I am — not only as a designer, but as a person. Translating self-awareness into visual form turned out to be harder than I expected, but it gave the whole system a foundation.

  • PersonalityEmpathetic, calm, thoughtful — approaching design with both heart and logic.
  • ValuesKindness · Clarity · Honesty · Sincerity.
  • USPA designer who blends structure with warmth — bringing empathy and calm clarity into every decision.
  • AudienceYoung audiences who value calm, thoughtful design — students, young professionals, and small creative brands that prefer sincerity over flashiness.
Brand discovery moodboardimages/identity-moodboard.png
03

Brand Message

I believe great design is more than aesthetics — it's about making people feel understood. I bring empathy and calm intention to every project, translating complex ideas into clear, approachable visuals.

Tone of voice: Calm, never rushed · Warm and sincere · Clear, without unnecessary complexity · Genuine and human.

04

Logo

The mark is built from my initials, "yw," as a minimal, balanced wordmark. The rounded, flowing letterforms express connection and empathy, while the geometric precision reflects logic and balance — two traits central to how I design.

A cobalt-blue circle gives the mark a calm, steady foundation: blue for trust and sincerity, the circle for wholeness and inclusiveness. Together they carry a quiet confidence — professional, but approachable.

The "yw" logoimages/identity-logo.png
05

Visual System

The system centers on cobalt blue to express clarity, trust, and sincerity, balanced with muted blue and off-white for warmth and quiet confidence.

Cobalt Blue#093180
Muted Blue#496091
Off-White#FFFEFA

Typeface — Quicksand. A rounded sans-serif for clarity, friendliness, and modern simplicity. Its soft geometry echoes the "yw" wordmark; bold weights add confidence, lighter weights keep text calm and readable.

06

Application

Finally, I applied the system to a stationery suite — carrying the logo, palette, and rounded supporting shapes across business cards and letterhead so the brand feels consistent everywhere it appears.

Stationery suiteimages/identity-stationery.png
07

Reflection

The hardest part wasn't the design — it was the discovery. Translating self-awareness into visual form forced me to articulate what I value and how I want to work, which is surprisingly difficult to do for yourself. Once I had a clear brand message, every visual decision became easier to justify.

I see this as an early stage of an evolving visual voice — something I'll keep refining as I grow. The biggest takeaway: a strong identity isn't about a clever mark, but about consistency and a point of view that holds up everywhere it appears.
